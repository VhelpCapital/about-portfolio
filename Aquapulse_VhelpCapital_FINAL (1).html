01 — The Problem

India's Aquaculture Value Chain Is Broken at Every Link.

India is the world's 2nd largest aquaculture nation — a $9.2 billion shrimp market with 30 million people in fish production. And yet, billions of dollars in value are destroyed every year through a fragmented, intelligence-free supply chain.

The losses aren't marginal. They're structural — embedded at every stage of the value chain, from the pond to the plate. When we examined the numbers, we were struck by how solvable these problems actually are with the right technology and operational model.

Pre-Harvest Failures
70%+ farmers lack credit — trapped in high-cost informal lending cycles that limit investment in quality inputs
80%+ farms without scientific guidance — productivity losses worth $1B+ annually from suboptimal feed, stocking, and harvest practices
Fragmented supply chain — low bargaining power and suppressed incomes for millions of small aquafarmers across coastal India
Post-Harvest Failures
20–30% shrimp spoilage = $2B+ loss annually — poor cold chain management and pre-processing delays
Only ~5% pre-cooled at harvest — 10.5% marine fish wasted = $1.5B+ in avoidable farmgate losses
Poor buyer access & <50% compliance — depressed price realization, ~$1B in lost value for India's aquaculture exporters annually

"India is the world's largest shrimp exporter — yet we waste 20–30% of every harvest. This isn't a supply problem. It's an intelligence problem."
